# Technical Indicators for Scalping

## Overview
Technical indicators help scalpers identify entry and exit points, confirm trends, and measure market conditions. For scalping, speed and simplicity are key - too many indicators cause analysis paralysis.

## Essential Moving Averages

### Exponential Moving Average (EMA)

#### EMA 9 (Fast)
- Quick trend direction
- Entry signal when price crosses
- Support/resistance in strong trends
- Best for M1-M5 timeframes

#### EMA 21 (Medium)
- Trend confirmation
- Pullback entry levels
- Dynamic support/resistance
- Works well with EMA 9

#### EMA 50 (Slow)
- Overall trend direction
- Major support/resistance
- Institutional level
- Trend filter

### MA Scalping Setups

#### EMA Crossover
- EMA 9 crosses EMA 21
- Direction of cross = trade direction
- Wait for pullback after cross
- Stop beyond the MAs

#### MA Ribbon
- Multiple EMAs (8, 13, 21, 34)
- Aligned = strong trend
- Spread apart = trending
- Converging = possible reversal

#### Price + MA Bounce
- Price touches EMA in trend
- Bounce candle forms
- Enter in trend direction
- Stop below EMA

## Momentum Oscillators

### RSI (Relative Strength Index)

#### Settings
- Period: 14 (standard) or 7 (faster for scalping)
- Overbought: 70
- Oversold: 30

#### Scalping Uses
- Below 30: Look for long entries
- Above 70: Look for short entries
- Divergence: Powerful reversal signal
- 50 level: Trend strength indicator

#### RSI Divergence Scalping
1. Price makes new high/low
2. RSI doesn't confirm
3. Wait for reversal candle
4. Enter opposite direction
5. Target previous swing

### Stochastic Oscillator

#### Settings
- %K: 14
- %D: 3
- Overbought: 80
- Oversold: 20

#### Scalping Uses
- Best in ranging markets
- Cross below 80: Short signal
- Cross above 20: Long signal
- Combine with support/resistance

### MACD

#### Settings
- Fast EMA: 12
- Slow EMA: 26
- Signal: 9

#### Scalping Signals
- MACD crosses signal line: Entry signal
- Histogram expanding: Momentum increasing
- Histogram contracting: Momentum decreasing
- Zero line cross: Trend change

## Volatility Indicators

### Bollinger Bands

#### Settings
- Period: 20
- Standard Deviation: 2

#### Scalping Uses

##### Band Bounce
- Price touches lower band in uptrend: Buy
- Price touches upper band in downtrend: Sell
- Confirm with reversal candle

##### Squeeze Setup
- Bands contract tightly
- Expect breakout
- Enter on band expansion
- Direction = first move out

##### Band Walk
- Price "walks" along band
- Strong trend indication
- Trade pullbacks to middle band
- Don't fade the trend

### ATR (Average True Range)

#### Settings
- Period: 14

#### Uses
- Measure volatility
- Set stop losses (1-2x ATR)
- Set take profits
- Compare to average for conditions

## Volume Indicators

### Volume

#### Basic Uses
- Confirm breakouts (high volume)
- Spot exhaustion (volume decline)
- Identify accumulation/distribution
- Best on stock/crypto charts

#### Volume Scalping Rules
- Breakout + high volume = valid
- Breakout + low volume = suspect
- Spike volume = possible reversal
- Declining volume in trend = weakening

### Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP)

#### Uses
- Intraday fair value
- Institutional reference point
- Support/resistance level
- Mean reversion target

#### VWAP Scalping
- Price above VWAP: Bullish bias
- Price below VWAP: Bearish bias
- Bounces off VWAP: Entry opportunity
- Return to VWAP: Mean reversion trades

## Support/Resistance Tools

### Pivot Points

#### Types
- Standard Pivots
- Fibonacci Pivots
- Camarilla Pivots

#### Scalping Uses
- Pre-calculated S/R levels
- Bounce trades at pivots
- Breakout trades through pivots
- Daily pivots most common

### Fibonacci Retracements

#### Key Levels
- 38.2%: Shallow retracement
- 50%: Medium retracement
- 61.8%: Deep retracement

#### Scalping Application
- Draw from swing to swing
- Enter at retracement levels
- Stop beyond 61.8% or 78.6%
- Target new high/low

## Indicator Combinations

### Simple Setup 1: Trend Trading
- EMA 21 (trend direction)
- RSI 14 (confirmation)
- Entry: Pullback to EMA, RSI confirming

### Simple Setup 2: Range Trading
- Bollinger Bands
- Stochastic Oscillator
- Entry: Band touch + Stochastic signal

### Simple Setup 3: Momentum
- EMA 9 and EMA 21
- MACD
- Entry: EMA cross + MACD confirmation

## Common Indicator Mistakes

### What to Avoid
- Using too many indicators
- Relying solely on indicators
- Ignoring price action
- Not understanding indicator math
- Using same settings for all timeframes
- Over-optimizing (curve fitting)

## Indicator Best Practices

### Keep It Simple
- Maximum 2-3 indicators
- Understand what each measures
- Use for confirmation, not prediction
- Price action is primary

### Types Should Complement
- One trend indicator (MA)
- One momentum indicator (RSI)
- One volume/volatility (ATR)
- Don't double up on same type

## Summary

Best indicators for scalping:
- **EMAs**: Trend direction and dynamic S/R
- **RSI**: Momentum and divergence
- **Bollinger Bands**: Volatility and mean reversion
- **VWAP**: Intraday fair value
- **ATR**: Stop loss and target calculation

Remember: Indicators confirm what price action shows. Never trade solely on indicator signals without price context.

# Scalping Entry Techniques

## Overview
Scalping is a trading style that aims to profit from small price movements, typically holding positions for seconds to minutes. Entry techniques are crucial as the margin for error is minimal in scalping.

## Core Entry Methods

### 1. Breakout Entries

#### Range Breakout
- Identify consolidation/range on M1-M5 timeframe
- Wait for candle to close outside range
- Enter in direction of breakout
- Stop loss inside the range
- Target 1-2x the range height

#### Level Breakout
- Mark significant support/resistance levels
- Wait for strong candle close through level
- Enter after first candle closes beyond level
- Stop loss below/above breakout candle
- Quick 5-10 pip target

### 2. Pullback Entries

#### Trend Pullback
- Identify clear trend direction
- Wait for minor pullback (2-3 candles against trend)
- Enter when price resumes trend direction
- Use EMA or previous swing for stop
- Ride to next swing high/low

#### Fibonacci Pullback
- After impulse move, draw Fibonacci
- Wait for retracement to 38.2% or 50%
- Enter on reversal candle at these levels
- Stop beyond 61.8% level
- Target new high/low

### 3. Bounce Entries

#### Support/Resistance Bounce
- Mark clear S/R levels
- Wait for price to test level
- Look for rejection candle (pin bar, engulfing)
- Enter on confirmation candle
- Tight stop beyond the level

#### Moving Average Bounce
- Use EMA 9 and EMA 21
- Wait for price to touch EMA in trending market
- Enter on bounce candle
- Stop below EMA
- Quick target 5-10 pips

## Candlestick Entry Signals

### Pin Bar/Hammer
- Long wick showing rejection
- Enter on close of candle
- Stop beyond the wick
- High probability reversal signal

### Engulfing Candle
- Current candle engulfs previous candle
- Shows strong momentum shift
- Enter on close
- Stop beyond engulfing candle

### Inside Bar Breakout
- Candle contained within previous candle
- Enter on break of inside bar high/low
- Stop on opposite side
- Good for volatile scalps

### Doji Reversal
- Doji at key level
- Shows indecision/potential reversal
- Wait for next candle confirmation
- Enter on confirmation close

## Indicator-Based Entries

### RSI Divergence
- Price makes new high/low
- RSI doesn't confirm
- Enter on reversal candle
- Powerful reversal signal

### MACD Signal Line Cross
- MACD line crosses signal line
- Enter in direction of cross
- Use with price action confirmation
- Good trend confirmation

### Bollinger Band Squeeze
- Bands contract tightly
- Enter on expansion (breakout)
- Direction determined by first move
- High volatility expected

### Stochastic Oversold/Overbought
- Below 20 = oversold (look to buy)
- Above 80 = overbought (look to sell)
- Wait for cross back before entry
- Works best in ranging markets

## Volume-Based Entries

### Volume Spike
- Sudden increase in volume
- Confirms strength of move
- Enter with the volume
- Indicates institutional activity

### Volume Divergence
- Price rises but volume decreases
- Warns of potential reversal
- Enter counter-trend on confirmation
- Risk management crucial

## Time-Based Entries

### Session Open Entries
- London/NY opens bring volatility
- Wait for first 15 minutes
- Enter on direction of initial move
- High probability moves

### News Fade
- After news spike settles
- Enter in opposite direction of spike
- Small target, tight stop
- Only after volatility decreases

## Entry Execution

### Market Orders
- Immediate execution
- Used when speed is critical
- Accept current spread
- Best for breakout trades

### Limit Orders
- Specify entry price
- Used for pullback entries
- May not get filled
- Better entry price

### Stop Orders
- Enter when price hits level
- Good for breakout trading
- Triggers automatically
- Ensures entry on breakout

## Entry Checklist

Before every scalp entry:
- [ ] Clear setup identified?
- [ ] Entry signal present?
- [ ] Stop loss determined?
- [ ] Target realistic (1:1 minimum)?
- [ ] Spread acceptable?
- [ ] No major news imminent?
- [ ] Risk within limits (0.5-1%)?

## Common Entry Mistakes

### What to Avoid
- Entering before signal completes
- Chasing price after missed entry
- Ignoring spread costs
- Trading during news events
- Over-sizing positions
- Not having a clear exit plan

## Best Entry Conditions

### Ideal Scalping Conditions
- Good volatility (not too low, not too high)
- Tight spreads
- Clear price action
- During active sessions
- No major news pending
- Trending or clearly ranging market

## Summary

Successful scalping entries require:
- Clear entry criteria
- Fast execution
- Tight risk management
- Active session timing
- Low spread awareness
- Discipline to wait for setups

Master 2-3 entry techniques rather than trying to use all of them. Consistency comes from specialization.

# Market Conditions for Scalping

## Overview
Understanding market conditions is crucial for scalping success. Different market environments require different strategies, and some conditions are simply not suitable for scalping.

## Types of Market Conditions

### 1. Trending Markets

#### Characteristics
- Clear directional movement
- Higher highs and higher lows (uptrend)
- Lower highs and lower lows (downtrend)
- Strong momentum candles
- Moving averages aligned in trend direction

#### How to Scalp
- Trade in direction of trend only
- Enter on pullbacks to moving averages
- Use breakouts of consolidations
- Larger targets possible
- Higher win rate when with trend

#### Best Indicators
- Moving averages (EMA 9, 21, 50)
- ADX > 25 confirms trend strength
- MACD histogram expanding

### 2. Ranging Markets

#### Characteristics
- Price bounces between support and resistance
- No clear directional bias
- ADX below 20
- Moving averages flat and intertwined
- Equal highs and lows forming

#### How to Scalp
- Fade the extremes (sell resistance, buy support)
- Smaller targets (middle of range)
- Tight stops beyond range boundaries
- Higher frequency of trades
- Works well with oscillators

#### Best Indicators
- RSI (oversold/overbought)
- Stochastic oscillator
- Bollinger Bands
- Support/resistance levels

### 3. Choppy/Whipsaw Markets

#### Characteristics
- Erratic price movement
- False breakouts common
- No follow-through on moves
- Quick reversals
- Difficult to read price action

#### How to Scalp
- AVOID TRADING
- Wait for conditions to change
- Reduce position size if must trade
- Use tighter stops
- Lower expectations

#### Recognition
- Multiple wicks on candles
- Price crossing MAs repeatedly
- Failed breakout patterns
- Expansion then immediate contraction

### 4. High Volatility Markets

#### Characteristics
- Large candles and ranges
- Fast price movement
- Wide spreads possible
- News-driven or event-driven
- Gaps and quick reversals

#### How to Scalp
- Reduce position size (50% or less)
- Wider stops required
- Larger potential targets
- Wait for volatility to settle
- Be very selective with entries

#### Caution
- Slippage more likely
- Spread widening
- Stop losses may gap through
- Emotional decision-making risk

### 5. Low Volatility Markets

#### Characteristics
- Small candles and ranges
- Slow price movement
- Tight consolidations
- Typically during off-hours
- Asian session for major pairs

#### How to Scalp
- Very difficult - consider avoiding
- Targets must be smaller
- Spread eats into profits
- Range trading only option
- Better to wait for volatility

## Session-Based Conditions

### Asian Session (20:00 - 00:00 EST)
- Low volatility for major pairs
- Better for JPY pairs
- Range-bound trading
- Good for marking levels for later
- Avoid EUR/USD and GBP/USD scalping

### London Session (02:00 - 11:00 EST)
- High volatility
- Strong trends develop
- Best for EUR and GBP pairs
- Breakouts of Asian range
- Prime scalping time

### New York Session (08:00 - 17:00 EST)
- Highest volume (overlap with London)
- News releases (8:30 AM)
- Trend continuation or reversals
- Good for USD pairs
- Peak scalping hours: 8:00-11:00

### Overlap Period (08:00 - 11:00 EST)
- London + New York both active
- Maximum liquidity
- Best spreads
- Strongest moves
- Ideal scalping conditions

## News Impact on Conditions

### High Impact News
- NFP, FOMC, CPI, GDP
- Avoid scalping 30 min before/after
- Extreme volatility
- Wide spreads
- Unpredictable moves

### Medium Impact News
- PMI, retail sales, employment
- Caution recommended
- Possible spike then reversal
- Watch spread widening

### Low Impact News
- Usually minimal impact
- Normal trading possible
- Stay aware but don't avoid

## Identifying Current Conditions

### Quick Assessment Process
1. Check ADX: >25 = trending, <20 = ranging
2. Look at MA alignment
3. Measure recent candle sizes
4. Check ATR vs average
5. Note time of day/session
6. Check economic calendar

### Condition Indicators

#### ATR (Average True Range)
- Above average = high volatility
- Below average = low volatility
- Helps set stops and targets

#### ADX (Average Directional Index)
- >25 = trending
- <20 = ranging
- Rising ADX = strengthening trend

#### Bollinger Bands
- Expanding = increasing volatility
- Contracting = decreasing volatility
- Squeeze = expect breakout

## Adapting Strategy to Conditions

### Strategy Matrix

| Condition | Strategy | Position Size | Stop Loss | Target |
|-----------|----------|--------------|-----------|--------|
| Trending | Pullbacks | Normal | Structure | Extended |
| Ranging | Fade extremes | Normal | Beyond range | Middle |
| Choppy | AVOID | Minimal | Tight | Minimal |
| High Vol | Selective | Reduced | Wide | Large |
| Low Vol | Wait/Range | Reduced | Tight | Small |

## Red Flags - Don't Scalp When

### Market Conditions
- [ ] Choppy, whipsaw action
- [ ] Very low volatility
- [ ] Major news pending (< 30 min)
- [ ] Spread unusually wide
- [ ] During market holidays
- [ ] First/last hour of week

### Personal Conditions
- [ ] Tired or emotional
- [ ] Distracted
- [ ] Already hit daily loss limit
- [ ] Trading unfamiliar pairs
- [ ] Poor internet connection

## Optimal Scalping Conditions

### Green Flags
- Clear trend or clean range
- Normal to slightly elevated volatility
- Tight spreads
- Active session time
- No imminent high-impact news
- Clean price action
- ATR within normal range

## Summary

Market conditions dictate scalping success:
- **Trending**: Trade pullbacks with trend
- **Ranging**: Fade extremes
- **Choppy**: DON'T TRADE
- **High Vol**: Reduce size, wider stops
- **Low Vol**: Wait or avoid

Best scalping occurs during London/NY overlap with clear trending or ranging conditions, tight spreads, and no imminent news. Always assess conditions before any scalping session.
